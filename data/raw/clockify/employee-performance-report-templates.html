What is a performance review?

\n

A performance review (also known as a performance evaluation or a performance appraisal) is a written assessment that contains all the details about an employee's contributions at work over a given time period.

\n

Managers conduct employee evaluations to review the performance of their employees regarding their:

\n
    \n
  • Strengths and weaknesses
    • \n
  • Skills and competencies
    • \n
  • Ability to meet deadlines
    • \n
  • Ability to work in a team
    • \n
  • Attendance history
    • \n
\n

Once managers have all the info about employees, they need to provide their workers with feedback. For example, if there are some particular areas for improvement, managers need to let employees know, so that corrections can be made.

\n Performance review: 6 common methods and self-evaluations\n

Types of performance reviews

\n

When evaluating the work of employees, reviewers can conduct appraisals by analyzing an individual performance over a specific period of time, such as a month or a longer time frame.

\n

Speaking of a person performing an appraisal, this is usually a manager or a team lead, but can also be coworkers and employees themselves, in the case of peer reviews and self-assessments.

\n

To show you the most common types of performance reviews, we will divide performance appraisals by the following parameters:

\n
    \n
  • Their frequency
    • \n
  • The person conducting the review
    • \n
\n

Let\u2019s see what each of these categories entails.

\n

Types of performance reviews by frequency

\n

When it comes to the frequency of performance appraisals, we can distinguish between:

\n
    \n
  • Monthly performance reviews
    • \n
  • Quarterly performance reviews
    • \n
  • Mid-year performance reviews
    • \n
  • Annual performance reviews
    • \n
\n

There are also weekly or fortnightly performance appraisals \u2014 although they\u2019re not as common as the types of reviews listed above.

\n

Monthly performance reviews

\n

Monthly performance reviews are especially practical when managers need to evaluate the work of:

\n
    \n
  • Newly hired employees \u2014 to review their performance during their first assignments.
    • \n
  • Employees working on a new project \u2014 to evaluate the work of all employees involved in a project.
    • \n
  • Employees on short-term contracts or freelancers \u2014 to review their work during the limited amounts of time during which they are employed.
    • \n
\n

Monthly performance reviews are a great way to make sure that workers stay on track with project progress and that their work is aligned with the company\u2019s goals.

\n

Quarterly performance reviews

\n

When doing quarterly appraisals, managers focus on employee performance over three months of the year. And, since there are 12 months in a year, there are four quarters \u2014 four quarterly reviews (Q1, Q2, Q3, and Q4).

\n

Each quarterly appraisal needs to include metrics such as:

\n
    \n
  • Goals
    • \n
  • Targets
    • \n
  • Budgets
    • \n
\n

Then, team leads can use these metrics to evaluate their employees.

\n

In addition, they can also review employee performance by highlighting employees\u2019 areas of excellence and areas that need improvement.

\n

Mid-year performance reviews

\n

Mid-year performance appraisals cover a six-month period and represent employee performance over these six months.

\n

The key advantage of conducting mid-year reviews is that it gives managers a better overview of:

\n
    \n
  • Employee past performance (over the last six months) \u2014 the tasks and activities that worked well and those that haven\u2019t, as well as the highlights of employee performance.
    • \n
  • Plans for the next six months \u2014 the precise methods that will help employees improve their critical areas. Managers can also see employee goals for the next six months along with a precise plan to achieve them.
    • \n
\n

Annual performance review

\n

Annual performance appraisals are a great way to evaluate employee work over a year.

\n

It\u2019s a summary of all the achievements an employee has managed to accomplish over the past year, as well as suggested areas for improvement.

\n

However, this type of review isn\u2019t suitable for all businesses, because it isn\u2019t always easy to sum up an entire year of work into one work performance review. So, instead of evaluating employees once a year, many companies opt for 360 Performance reviews \u2014 which we\u2019ll explain in the next section.

\n

Types of performance reviews by the reviewer

\n

Now, when it comes to the person performing a review, it doesn\u2019t always have to be a manager.

\n

Here are some other types of reviews that don\u2019t entirely (or at all) include team leads:

\n
    \n
  • 360 Performance review \u2014 managers and coworkers perform such appraisals
    • \n
  • Peer review \u2014 teammates evaluate other colleagues
    • \n
  • Self-assessment \u2014 employees review their own work
    • \n
\n

360 Performance review

\n

360 Performance appraisals are reviews that gather information about employees from their managers and coworkers from their team.

\n

Aside from employee performance, this type of evaluation focuses on reviewing areas such as:

\n
    \n
  • Accountability and work habits
    • \n
  • Trust and honesty
    • \n
  • Teamwork
    • \n
  • Interpersonal communication
    • \n
  • Leadership
    • \n
\n

A 360 Performance review is invaluable because it provides feedback about individual performance from all directions.

\n

Peer review

\n

Unlike with the 360 Performance review, in a Peer review, teammates are solely the ones who review employee performance.

\n

Here are the key areas this review needs to cover:

\n
    \n
  • Ability to work in a team
    • \n
  • Ability to collaborate and communicate with other teammates
    • \n
  • Trust and respect
    • \n
  • Accountability
    • \n
\n

This type of feedback is important because it can reveal particular skills, or strengths and weaknesses that managers wouldn\u2019t be able to figure out themselves. After all, it\u2019s only logical that team members who are constantly collaborating will be able to understand and assess each others\u2019 way of work from a different angle than managers.

\n

Self-assessment review

\n

A self-assessment review ensures that employees reflect on their performance over a set period of time.

\n

When filling out a self-assessment review, employees should think of examples of good and poor performance.

\n

Moreover, they also need to figure out what barriers stand in their way of achieving results, and find ways to overcome these barriers.

\n

Finally, workers should think about the metrics they\u2019ll use to measure their performance \u2014 and ensure that they\u2019re on track.

Why are performance reviews important?

\n

Here are the key reasons why job performance reviews are significant for both managers and employees.

\n

Performance reviews ensure personal roles are linked with business goals

\n

Performance appraisals are significant because they show whether individual roles are aligned with business vision and goals.

\n

In simpler terms, it\u2019s important that employees understand the bigger picture \u2014 i.e. how their role within the company affects the company's vision and goals.

\n

For example, content writers need to understand that the blog posts they are writing are a vital part of the company\u2019s marketing strategy, which further affects the overall success of a company. So, by creating quality content, content writers are helping increase the visibility of the company and driving more organic traffic to the company\u2019s website, and are thus helping accomplish business goals in the long run.

\n

And, it\u2019s up to managers to make sure that all employees know what their responsibilities and roles are. That way, workers can give their best to accomplish overall targets.

\n

And, when appraisals show that employee performance doesn\u2019t contribute to the bigger picture, managers need to let employees know that.

\n

When everyone\u2019s on the same page when it comes to job roles and responsibilities, it\u2019s easier both for employees and managers to perform better.

\n

Performance reviews provide regular feedback about work

\n

Providing employees with regular feedback will help them see what their strengths and weaknesses are. Performance appraisals are a great way to include feedback about someone\u2019s work.

\n

Besides, employees will know what is expected of them, so they\u2019ll be able to act accordingly.

\n

This also increases transparency within the organization and improves communication among employees and their managers.

\n

Performance reviews help workers plan their career better

\n

Performance reviews can help employees set precise objectives as a reference point in their career.

\n

These objectives will allow employees to develop their skills and advance their career in the long run.

\n

In addition, performance appraisals will reveal any areas that need improvement. Then, if needed, employees can request training from their mentors, to gain the ability to reach their set goals.

\n Free goal tracker app\n

Performance reviews ensure that employees are rewarded for good work

\n

When employees work hard and achieve great results, this will be reflected in their performance appraisals.

\n

In such cases, managers need to make sure to include attributes like \u201cexceeds expectations\u201d when describing employee performance.

\n

As a result, seeing such appraisals will surely motivate employees to continue with the good work. Besides, they\u2019ll know that all their efforts and long hours were worth it.

How do you evaluate employees?

\n

If you\u2019re a manager, and you\u2019re wondering how you should evaluate your employees, read on.

\n

We\u2019ll guide you through all the essential steps.

\n

Step 1: Read employee job descriptions

\n

The first step is reviewing job descriptions for each employee. That way, you\u2019ll make sure you know the responsibilities of each team member.

\n

Then, it\u2019ll be easier for you to compare employee performance with these benchmarks.

\n

Next, write down what employees generally do well and what areas need improvement.

\n

Note down the examples that show when employees exceeded their expectations.

\n

Step 2: Emphasize exceptional areas

\n

The next step is to find out the areas where employees have significantly improved their performance. Try to be specific and use examples if you can.

\n

In the case you have previous performance reviews for particular employees, make sure to compare them with the current appraisal.

\n

Additionally, if some employees have completed a course, a training, or have earned certifications, be sure to include these details in your performance review.

\n

Step 3: Evaluate strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats

\n

This step entails using previous appraisals to review employee strengths and weaknesses.

\n

Then, think about how these traits have changed since the previous review.

\n

You can use the SWOT framework for this step:

\n
    \n
  • S \u2014 strength
    • \n
  • W \u2014 weakness
    • \n
  • O \u2014 opportunities
    • \n
  • T \u2014 threat
    • \n
\n

Evaluating strengths

\n

Consider all the strengths of each employee, such as accomplishments and their expertise.

\n

For example, sales representatives need to make 20 sales each week. So, if some employees manage to make 30 sales per week, these sales reps are surely exceeding expectations by setting higher standards for themselves.

\n

Thus, proactivity is their key strength.

\n

Evaluating weaknesses

\n

Think about an employee\u2019s weaknesses as well, and include solutions to such issues, if you can.

\n

For instance, if you think that some workers need to improve their communication skills, you can suggest having a meeting with this person and brainstorming possible solutions together. Or, an HR team can organize a communication training session for the entire team.

\n

Evaluating opportunities

\n

Next, find out how employees can use their positive attributes to excel \u2014 i.e. their opportunities.

\n

For example, an employee is a quick learner and able to finish tasks on time. This shows managers that they can count on such workers when looking for someone to join a vital project.

\n

Evaluating threats

\n

Finally, note down possible threats that can happen along the way, so that employees can find a way to prevent them.

\n

For example, if workers struggle with communicating their messages clearly, provide them with some practical tips on how to create transparent messages. That way, you\u2019ll increase the chances to prevent any potential misunderstandings.

\n

Step 4: Suggest actions that can improve employee performance

\n

As their manager, you should empower your employees to enhance their performance, which will allow them to advance their career in the long run.

\n

You can recommend particular goals that will be beneficial to your employees, along with ways to accomplish these targets. Just remember that these goals need to be aligned with the company's values and vision.

\n

For instance, you can encourage your employees to set SMART goals:

\n
    \n
  • S \u2014 Specific
    • \n
  • M \u2014 Measurable
    • \n
  • A \u2014 Attainable
    • \n
  • R \u2014 Relevant
    • \n
  • T \u2014 Time-bound
    • \n
\n

This way, employees will find it easier to set goals for the future. Additionally, they will have a clearer picture of what their plans for improving performance should look like.

\n How to set SMART goals (+ examples and templates)\n

Step 5: Provide employees with feedback

\n

Always be sure to include constructive feedback in your performance reviews.

\n

That means that even when employees have some areas that need to be improved, remember to give suggestions on how to improve such areas.

\n

For example, what tactics they should use, or whether they should change the way that they perform particular tasks.

\n

And, of course, when it comes to areas where employees excel, make sure to highlight these achievements, too.

\n

Step 6: Encourage employee input

\n

Finally, let your employees know that they have an opportunity to share their thoughts on employee reviews, too.

\n

So, if there\u2019s anything they didn\u2019t quite understand, or they\u2019d like to discuss with you, encourage them to do so.

\n

It\u2019s vital that employees feel included because, that way, they\u2019ll be more motivated to perform better and accomplish the goals you\u2019ve set out for them.

\n

What do you write in an employee evaluation?

\n

If you\u2019re wondering how to write a performance review and what areas to include, we\u2019ll now guide you through some vital steps.

\n

Here are the most crucial areas employee evaluations should focus on:

\n
    \n
  • Achieving goals
    • \n
  • Leadership qualities
    • \n
  • Communication skills
    • \n
  • Teamwork
    • \n
  • Culture and values
    • \n
\n

Let\u2019s explore each area in more detail. We\u2019ll also provide you with the phrases you can use in your appraisals.

\n

Achieving goals

\n

You need to evaluate whether employees set challenging goals for themselves and how they deal with obstacles that prevent them from achieving these goals.

\n

In addition, it\u2019s important to review if employees help other coworkers accomplish their targets, too.

\n

When it comes to the exact wording you can use for this area, here\u2019s an idea from the book Effective Phrases for Performance Appraisals by James E. Neal:

\n

\"Achieves optimal levels of performance with/for\u2026\"

\n

Additionally, when talking about improvement, you can use phrases like:

\n

\"Continues to grow and improve\u2026\"

\n

and

\n

\"...is continuously planning for improvement\u2026\"

\n

Leadership qualities

\n

To review someone\u2019s leadership skills, you need to consider whether employees take into accounts other people\u2019s ideas when making decisions.

\n

It\u2019s also crucial to review if employees are willing to help each other with any work-related problems.

\n

Speaking of the wording, here\u2019s a suggestion from the book we previously mentioned:

\n

\"Provides support during periods of organizational change\u2026\"

\n

Communication skills

\n

Communication skills are essential in the workplace, which is why they need to be included in performance reviews.

\n

You need to evaluate if employees communicate clearly and transparently with others and whether they listen actively.

\n

You can use phrases included in the aforementioned book, like:

\n

\"Effectively communicates expectations\u2026\"

\n

\"Excels in facilitating group discussions\u2026\"

\n

Teamwork

\n

As their manager, you need to review if employees collaborate well with other team members.

\n

Besides, it\u2019s also important how they provide and receive feedback within the team.

\n

Another thing to have in mind when evaluating teamwork skills is whether employees treat other coworkers with respect.

\n

You can use phrases like:

\n

\u201cEffectively collaborates with other team members\u2026\u201d

\n

\u201cActively participates in work-related discussions\u2026\u201d

\n

Culture and values

\n

It\u2019s also important to think about company values and culture, and whether employees share the same values.

\n

When employees respect the same values as the company does, it\u2019ll be much easier for everyone to get along with each other.

\n

You can include phrases such as:

\n

\u201cPromotes company values every day\u2026\u201d

\n

\u201cNurtures a positive work environment\u2026\u201d
